Frequently asked questions

40 questions & answers about what Alpha Ticket Manager does, who it is for and what to discuss before installation.

Product overview

What is Alpha Ticket Manager?

Alpha Ticket Manager is a ticket issuing and management system for entertainment and cultural venues, events, museums, sports events and generally any business or body that needs organised ticket distribution. It covers box-office issuing, online bookings, seat selection, card payments, an electronic ticket with QR code, entrance control and sales reports.

How is it different from a simple cash register or an Excel file?

A simple cash register or an Excel file can help temporarily, but it doesn't fully organise the ticket experience. Alpha Ticket Manager connects box office, online booking, ticket types, seats, QR check-in and reports in one unified flow. This reduces double bookings, entrance errors, uncertainty about sales and manual work.

Is it a product only for large organisations?

No. The product's philosophy is to offer practical ticketing to small and medium venues, without the complexity and cost of a heavy enterprise platform. It can be used by a small theatre, a local cinema, a cultural club, a museum, a municipality or an organiser of sports and music events.

What is the core promise of the service?

The core promise is simple: from booking to the gate, everything organised. The customer finds or buys a ticket, receives it in physical or electronic form, shows it at the entrance, and the body has a clear view of sales, revenue, seats, available tickets and access control.

Can it work without online bookings?

Yes. Alpha Ticket Manager can be used only for local ticket issuing at one or more box offices (Simple edition). The online function can be added when the body is ready or when there is a need for online ticket pre-sales.

Can it work as online-only ticketing?

Yes, in specific scenarios the emphasis can be on online booking and payment. However, a simple computer is required to design and run the events shown online and to consolidate sales. Still, the product's strength is the combination of online and physical box office, covering both online buyers and walk-up visitors at the same time.

Use cases & industries

Which venues is it suitable for?

It is suitable for theatres, cinemas, museums, galleries, exhibition spaces, archaeological sites, cultural centres, municipal halls, sports events, stadiums, concerts, festivals, live stages, conferences, guided tours, tourism activities and small cruises or organised excursions.

Is it suitable for theatres?

Yes. Theatres need a performance schedule, seat selection, different ticket types, bookings, invitations, online pre-sales and fast entrance control. Alpha Ticket Manager can organise these elements in a way that is simple for the cashier and clear for the audience.

Is it suitable for cinemas?

Yes. A cinema can manage multiple halls, different screening times, online pre-sales, seat selection, ticket categories and revenue reports. The customer can pick a screening and seat, pay electronically and arrive with a QR ticket.

Can it be used in museums and exhibition spaces?

Yes. For museums, galleries, exhibitions and visitor attractions it can work with simple admission issuing, regular and reduced tickets, free admissions, group packages, school visits and QR entrance control. It can also support special events or visit time-slots.

Can it cover sports events or stadiums?

Yes. For sports events it can support day tickets, entry zones, general admission without numbered seats, or seat/gate management where required. QR control at the entrance helps with fast access and limiting double ticket use.

Is it useful for tourism activities?

Yes. Tourism offices, organised tours, day excursions, small cruises or seasonal activities can use the system for online and onsite ticket sales, especially in high-demand periods where a clear view of availability is needed.

Ticket issuing & box office

How is a ticket issued at the box office?

In the simple POS mode the cashier selects quantity, ticket type and payment method and proceeds to issue. The process is simple and can be done in one click! In a venue with numbered seats they select date, event, hall and specific seats before completing issuing and payment.

Are different ticket types supported?

Yes. Regular, reduced, free, student, child, accessibility, invitations, group packages or other categories the body needs can be created and designed. Each type can have a different price and appear clearly at the box office or in the online booking.

Can it be used without numbered seats?

Yes. The application doesn't require seats. It can work as a simple ticket box office for general admission, museums, exhibitions, concerts, sports events, festivals and venues where the number of tickets matters rather than the specific seat.

Can it be used with more than one box office?

The basic Simple edition can cover one box office, while a more complete installation can support several issuing points or workstations. This should be mapped out before the quote, so that licences, equipment, support and workflow are calculated correctly.

Is physical ticket printing supported?

Yes. The system can print physical tickets, usually with 58/80mm thermal printers for fast and economical issuing. Support for special barcode or label printers can also be discussed, depending on the ticket type and the venue's needs.

Can it support selling products besides tickets?

Yes, with proper configuration simple products or services sold at the box office can be registered, such as prints, performance programmes, souvenirs or special packages. The exact behaviour should be agreed based on the venue's operating model.

Online bookings & payments

How does online booking work?

The visitor enters the public booking page, selects a date, event, ticket type or seats, fills in their details and completes the card payment where enabled. After completion they receive the electronic ticket by email.

Can the customer select a seat online?

Yes, provided the event has a configured hall with seats. The customer can see availability and choose specific seats. For events without seats, online booking can be done by selecting quantity and ticket type.

Is the online ticket sent by email?

Yes. After completing the online process, the customer receives an electronic ticket by email, usually with a QR code. If the installation includes tax integration, sending the required tax document can also be provided, depending on the agreed solution.

Are card payments supported?

Yes. The product can support card payments for online bookings and, depending on the installation, for local box-office issuing. Card payments require the appropriate commercial and technical setup with the payment provider used.

Can it work without an intermediary ticket marketplace?

Yes. The philosophy of Alpha Ticket Manager is to give the body its own organised online operation, so it sells tickets through its own environment — see the demo at ticketmanager.gr/demo — without necessarily depending on a large third-party marketplace or paying extra commissions.

Can there be online booking on my site?

Yes, online booking can be embedded into your existing site, so a prospective customer can try the flow: date selection, event selection, seat or ticket selection, entering details and viewing the e-ticket logic. Online booking, managed with minimal clicks on your side, especially helps sales because it shows how simple the product is.

Seats, halls & schedule

How are halls and seats created?

The administrator can define a hall with rows, columns, aisles and gaps, so the layout resembles the real space. After creating the layout, the seats can be used in events or screenings.

Can there be many halls?

Yes. The application can support multiple halls with different capacity and layout, particularly useful for cinemas, cultural centres, multi-spaces and organisations with different event venues.

How is the events schedule organised?

The schedule can include event title, hall, dates, times and active/inactive status. So the body can organise screenings, performances, recurring events or special events and define what appears at the box office or online.

Can one event have many dates and times?

Yes. This is a basic need for cinemas, theatres and festivals. The same play or event can have different times or dates, with separate availability and sales per screening or performance.

Can a ticket limit be set without seats?

Yes. For general-admission events or activities without numbered seats, a capacity or available number of tickets can be set, so no more than allowed are sold for the venue or event.

QR check-in, security & entrance control

How is entrance control done?

Control can be done by scanning the QR code or entering the ticket number. The system checks whether the ticket is valid, whether it is for the correct event or date and whether it has already been used. This reduces the risk of double entry or incorrect checking.

What happens if someone tries to use the same ticket a second time?

The system can show that the ticket has already been checked or used. This is critical for online tickets, where the customer may have the file on a phone or printout. QR control offers better security than a simple visual check.

Can there be different users, such as cashier and controller?

Yes. The application can support distinct roles, such as administrator, cashier and gate controller. This helps with access control and simplifies daily operation, as each user sees the functions they need.

Is special equipment needed for QR control?

A suitable QR scanner or a device that supports scanning can be used. The exact equipment depends on the installation environment, the entry intensity and the number of gates. For large events it is important to plan the right number of control stations.

Reports, customers & administration

What reports does Alpha Ticket Manager offer?

It offers reports on sales, revenue, payment methods, ticket types, event, hall and time period. So the manager can see what was sold, when, how and for which event. The reports help with administration, accounting checks and planning future events.

Can data be exported for processing?

Yes. Export of data to files suitable for further processing, such as CSV or other office formats, can be provided, depending on the edition and configuration. This is useful for accountants, management, event reviews and internal analyses.

Is a customer database or visitor data supported?

Yes. The application can keep basic customer data, such as full name, email and phone, especially for online bookings and tax needs. Use of the data must always respect personal-data protection rules and properly inform the visitor.

Installation, support & commercial topics

What does the Simple edition include?

The Simple edition can cover the basic ticket issuing and organisation for one box office, without extra equipment, without technical support services, without online functionality or tax integrations. The final proposal should be shaped according to your needs.

Can existing equipment be used?

Yes, in many cases equipment the customer already has can be used, such as a computer, thermal printer, cash register or peripherals. Before the quote, the existing equipment should be recorded and compatibility checked. You can easily send us a list or photos of your existing equipment so we can ensure smooth operation and compatibility.

Does it support a tax process, cash register or provider?

Alpha Ticket Manager can be designed to support different tax issuing scenarios, such as ticket-only issuing, integration with a cash register, or use of an e-invoicing provider, depending on the customer's needs and status. Because tax requirements differ, the scenario must be confirmed before implementation.

How does an interested customer start?

The interested party can request a demo and describe their current way of operating: type of venue, number of box offices, need for online booking, ticket types, whether seats exist, existing equipment and tax issuing method. With this information a proposal can be shaped that covers their real needs.
